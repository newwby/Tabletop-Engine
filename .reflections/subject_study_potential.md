# Project learning evaluation as a reflective blog subject

**Date recorded**: 05/04/2026
**Last updated**: 05/04/2026

This reflection covered the potential of the project, investigating agentic development to build a tabletop campaign, as study and blog material. It then expands into my personal observed benefits, maps to known research, and means to critically evaluate without harming learning depth.

## Anecdotal analysis

### Themes for reflection

Agentic development was framed as a meaningful topic for reflective writing because it changes where effort sits in the development process.

Agent development appears to shift work toward prompt design, task structuring, orchestration, review, evaluation, and iterative refinement. Research in the field suggests that developers overestimate their productivity gains utilising agents for development, supporting the hypothesis that it changes where the work lays.


### Benefits

My personal experience is that multi-agent workflows fit my working style well, as they enable setting an agent on a task, reviewing another in parallel, and maintaining momentum across several work threads.

Other themes identified were:

* improved thinking precision through careful prompting
* parallel cognition improvements
* increased exposure to review and analysis
* faster comprehension of content that is not self-authored
* more detailed work output

### Risks

During exploratory phases there are potential issues with agent development that must be considered.

* Illusion of producitvity (slowdown)
* Over-structuring early work
* Cognitive fragmentation (shallow focus/context switching)
* Over-reliance on generated output
* Verbosity does not equal quality
* Skill gap risk

### Evidence to support anecdotal analysis

Evidence exists to support that the above themes are reflected in broader research around AI-assisted development. This was especially present for:

* iterative refinement
* human-in-the-loop evaluation
* feedback-driven workflows
* multi-step orchestration
* shifts from pure implementation towards oversight and judgement

Evidence suggests that explicit prompting encourages clearer problem framing and multi-agent workflows support task decomposition. AI-assisted development shifts work emphasis towards reviewing and evaluating unfamiliar output, and proactive questioning of prompting/output aligns with planning behaviour.

Productivity is difficult to measure because perceived speed and output quality do not always align. Research commonly shows a gap in producitvity reality vs perception. Though speed may not be improved, outputs remain better structured and end-to-end complete.

---

## Learning Risks

There are risks that agentic development can reduce learning depth in unfamiliar subjects if it replaces productive struggle too often. By writing less code and experiencing fewer problems with output, an illusion of understanding can be reached.

Agentic development provides exposure to structural review that is not reproducable working alone. This produces evaluation-oriented habits such as faster code reading, proactive questioning, and better workflow comprehension, which cannot be achieved through traditional prototyping and learning practices (such as stack overflow, googling, and manual experimentation).

Therefore, one solution would be to continue experimenting without artificial intelligence, preserving fundamentals and struggle depth, whilst working in separate projects with agents. Evaluation skill must remain grounded in the ability to independently explain and reproduce work being evaluated.

### Terms
* **Struggle depth** is the learning that happens when you get stuck long enough to build mental models, instinct, and edge-case intuition.
* **Illusion of understanding** refers to being able to recognise, follow, and review solutions without being capable of deriving or reconstructing them.
